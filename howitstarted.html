How it Started

31.10.2008 - An anonymous person by the name of Satoshi Nakamoto publishes the Bitcoin (BTC) whitepaper.

Three months later, Satoshi makes the first Bitcoin transaction and sends 10 BTC to computer scientist Hal Finney.

22.5.10 - the first official documented purchase of goods using Bitcoin, when two pizzas were bought for 10,000 BTC.


Satoshi's Vision:
At the core of Bitcoin is a purely peer-to-peer version of electronic cash that allows online payments to be sent directly from one party to another without the burdens of going through a financial institution.

The total supply of Bitcoin is fixed, with the number of Bitcoins capped at 21 million.

While we can mine more copper, or print more dollars, there will never be more than 21 million Bitcoins. Which is what earned Bitcoin the nicknames "hard money" and "digital gold".

The current supply of Bitcoin is around 19 million, mining near 90% of the entire supply. On average 900 Bitcoins are mined each day.

The last bitcoin will be mined in 2140, making Bitcoin completely deflationary. There will be no more bitcoins generated after this point; nevertheless they will be divisible.


Fast Forward to Today:
7.9.2021 - El Salvador became the first country to use Bitcoin as legal tender. That means it can be used to pay for everything: taxes, debts, groceries. And that there will no longer be capital gains taxes assessed on Bitcoin conversions.

Adoption by companies like Paypal, and Elon Musk's plan to integrate cryptocurrency payments into Twitter are just some of the latest news about the global adoption of cryptographic currencies and blockchain technology.
